Who I Am

Engineer. Builder.
Problem Solver.

I'm a fourth year Electrical Engineering student at the University of British Columbia , pursuing a Biomedical Engineering concentration. My academic journey has spanned over circuit design, embedded systems, MEMS fabrication, PLC automation, control systems, and biomedical instrumentation, and more.

Based in Vernon, BC, I've worked on everything from reverse engineering industrial PCBs under manufacturing constraints, to designing soft robotic actuators powered by hydraulic pressure, to studying MEMS chevron actuators fabricated with an in house SU8 process. Each project has pushed me to integrate engineering principles across multiple disciplines.

My career goal is to contribute to the development of medical devices and biomedical instrumentation systems at the intersection of hardware precision and human impact. I'm drawn to roles where electrical engineering directly supports clinical or quality-of-life outcomes.

Outside the lab, I hold certifications in industrial PLC programming and robotics simulation through FESTO, and I've applied those skills through hands on design of electropneumatic systems and HMI development using TIA Portal.
