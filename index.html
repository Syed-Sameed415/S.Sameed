Who I Am

Engineering And
Medicine.

I'm a fourth-year Electrical Engineering student (Biomedical Concentration) at the University of British Columbia. My academic journey has taken me across circuit design, embedded systems, MEMS fabrication, control systems, and bioinstrumentation.

Based in Vernon, BC, I've worked on everything from industrial PCB design with real manufacturing constraints, to soft robotics and MEMS actuators, to consumer health adjacent devices. I thrive in interdisciplinary teams where engineering, biology, and user experience collide.

My career aspiration is to contribute to the development of medical devices and biomedical instrumentation. I'm drawn to roles where my electrical engineering foundation supports meaningful clinical or quality-of-life outcomes.

Outside the lab, I value clear communication, structured problem-solving, and the kind of teamwork that makes iteration genuinely productive. I hold certifications in industrial PLCs and robotics, and I'm comfortable working from schematic to prototype to validation.
